Syllabus Overview

Set Theory & Logic

Fundamental principles including sets, relations, functions, and the basics of propositional and predicate logic.

Combinatorics

Counting techniques, permutations, combinations, pigeonhole principle, and an introduction to recurrence relations.

Graph Theory

Concepts of graphs and trees, including connectivity, cycles, Eulerian and Hamiltonian paths, and applications in network analysis.

Optimization Techniques

An overview of optimization in discrete settings: linear programming, integer programming, and methods for solving optimization problems.

Boolean Algebra

Study of Boolean functions, logic gates, and techniques for simplifying Boolean expressions with applications in digital circuit design.

Advanced Topics

Recurrence relations, generating functions, and their applications in solving complex problems in computer science.
